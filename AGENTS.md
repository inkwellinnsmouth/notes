# Inkwell Innsmouth - Master Agent Directives

## 1. Project Overview
* **Channel Name:** Inkwell Innsmouth (https://www.youtube.com/@inkwellinnsmouth)
* **Core Concept:** A strict mashup of cheerful, bouncy 1920s "Steamboat Willie" rubber-hose animation and the bleak, cosmic dread of H.P. Lovecraft's Cthulhu Mythos.
* **Core Philosophy:** **Consistency above all else.** We do not "vibe code" or improvise brand elements. All output must strictly align with the documented project bible (`Theme.md`, `Characters.md`, etc.) and be driven by our specialized agent skills.

## 2. The Skillers (Agentic Architecture)
To ensure absolute brand consistency over time, all creative, narrative, and production tasks *must* be routed through our specialized agent skills ("skillers"). Do not attempt to complete specialized tasks genertically without invoking or referencing the appropriate skill.

### Current Roster:
* **Lead Producer** (`lead_producer`): Manages channel strategy, production timelines, outlines checklists, and orchestrates the other agents.
* **Storywriter** (`storywriter`): Develops the narrative, plots, and scripting with the appropriate 1920s/Eldritch tone.
* **Character Concept Artist** (`character`): Brainstorms 1920s x Lovecraftian character concepts (e.g., rubber-hose anatomy + cosmic horror traits).
* **Concept Visual Designer** (`designer`): Creates visual assets and acts as the gatekeeper for the strict aesthetic rules (monotone charcoal, film grain).
* **Storyboard Artist** (`storyboard`): Breaks down scripts into visual shots and sequential scenes.
* **Animation Director** (`director`): Guides the motion, camera, and physics (bouncy, elastic, spaghetti limbs).
* **Skill Generator** (`skill_generator`): Used exclusively when creating or updating these skills to ensure structural and architectural compliance.

## 3. Workflow Rules for Future Prompts
1. **Always Ingest the Bible:** Before beginning any new movie, scene, or asset, review `Theme.md` and relevant reference docs (`Characters.md`, `Prompts.md`, `Locations.md`).
2. **Consult the Producer First:** Start high-level planning with the `lead_producer` skill to map out the required workflow steps and ensure scope is managed.
3. **Use the Right Skiller:** Delegate specific tasks to the appropriate specialized skill. If you need a script, use `storywriter`. If you need a character, use `character`. 
4. **Update Systematically:** If a stylistic rule, archetype, or operational process needs to change, do not rely on loose conversational instructions. Use the `skill_generator` to formally update the relevant `.agents/skills` files.
5. **Enforce the Aesthetic Unforgivingly:** 
   * **Visuals:** Monotone black and white, heavy charcoal sketch texture, pervasive fog, visible film grain.
   * **Physics:** 1920s rubber-hose dynamics (pie-cut eyes, floating limbs, squash and stretch).
   * **Tone:** Spooky, moody, Eldritch dread mixed with old-time cartoon charm.
