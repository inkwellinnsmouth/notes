**Working Title:** Barker's Morning Commute
**Logline:** Barker Blot wakes up in his dilapidated boarding house, gets ready for the day with a cheerful 1920s spring in his step, and drives his sputtering jalopy towards Innsmouth, only for the fog to reveal the terrifying cosmic reality of his route.

**Act 1: The Setup (The 1920s Trope)**
- Barker's alarm clock rings (it's a jagged, screaming mouth), he hits it with a comical oversized mallet, and does a bouncy, squash-and-stretch morning routine (shaving in a cracked mirror, whistling a silent-era ragtime tune).
- He steps out of his leaning, heavy-charcoal-sketch boarding house, trips all the way down the front stairs, but effortlessly turns it into a perfectly choreographed Charleston dance, landing smoothly in the driver's seat of his sputtering Model-T jalopy.

**Act 2: The Twist (The Eldritch Reveal)**
- The jalopy bounces happily down the dirt road (The Path Into Innsmouth) until the thick coastal fog rolls in, muting the bouncy ragtime score into a discordant, echoing drone.
- A mundane pot-hole in the road suddenly splits open into a massive, abyssal chasm lined with impossible teeth. The jalopy screech-halts, its headlights widening in terror like pie-cut eyes.

**Act 3: The Climax (Rubber-hose Horror)**
- Barker, terrified, melts entirely into a puddle of black ink on the floorboards, leaving only his flat cap suspended in mid-air and his eyes darting frantically.
- A colossal, shadowy tentacle sweeps out of the mist, gently picking up the entire jalopy (with puddle-Barker inside) and carelessly depositing it on the other side of the chasm before sinking back into the fog. Barker's body zips back into solid form under his hat, he tips his cap to the fog in polite thanks, and cheerfully continues his bouncy drive into the looming silhouette of Innsmouth.
