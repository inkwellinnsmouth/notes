**Name:** Worm Monster (unnamed)
**The 1920s Archetype:** The Mindless Grotesque — a lumbering, rubbery creature played for both horror and bleak cartoon slapstick.
**The Eldritch Twist:** A towering subterranean worm entity drawn from Lovecraftian mythology — cousin to the Dholes and Worms of the Earth. Vast, blind, and ancient, it does not think so much as hunger. Its very presence warps the ground, causing cobblestones and floorboards to buckle and ripple like water.
**Visual Design:** A massive, segmented worm rendered in the rubber-hose style — its body a series of bloated, overlapping rings of thick charcoal ink. Its "face" is a wet, gaping mouth ringed with concentric rows of needle teeth, surrounded by a fringe of stubby, wriggling feeler-tentacles. No eyes — just smooth, rubbery head flesh above the maw. The body texture is slick with ink wash and cross-hatched shading suggesting slime and ancient rot. Scale is deliberately absurd: it can fill an alleyway or emerge from a sewer grate with comedic rubber-hose elasticity before doing something horrifying.
**Signature Animation Gag:** When it bursts through the ground, a beat passes — then its rear end pops out of the ground on the other side of the street, looks around in confusion, then zips back in.
**Role in Innsmouth:** A recurring environmental hazard. It doesn't pursue characters with intelligence — it simply moves through the earth and anything unfortunate enough to be in its path. The townsfolk treat its occasional eruptions the way one treats a bad drain — an inconvenience, not an emergency.
