# Inkwell Innsmouth - Location Lore

This document catalogs the key locations in the Inkwell Innsmouth universe. All locations must adhere to the core aesthetic (monotone, warped rubber-hose perspective, high-contrast charcoal black & white).

## The Soggy Square (Town Center)
**The 1920s Archetype:** The Bustling Main Street.
**The Eldritch Twist:** The cobblestones aren't stone; they are smooth, calcified "scales" that ripple when stepped on. The town clock doesn't have numbers—it has thirteen shifting, weeping eyes, and the "Tick-Tock" sound is a wet, rhythmic heartbeat that slows down the closer you get to the water.
**Visual Design:** Buildings like the "Marsh Refinery" lean inward at impossible, "rubber-hose" angles, nearly touching at the top to blot out the moon. Thick black ink "pools" in the gutters. Massive, silhouetted tentacles are woven into the wrought-iron streetlamps, which flicker with a dim, sickly luminescence.
**Signature Gag Elements:** When the town clock "strikes" the hour, the entire street "squashes" flat like a stepped-on accordion, then "stretches" back up, causing all pedestrians to bounce three feet into the air.

## The "Gilman House" Hotel
**The 1920s Archetype:** The Grand (but Seedy) Hotel.
**The Eldritch Twist:** The hotel is a literal labyrinth of shifting ink. The hallways stretch infinitely if you walk too fast, and the wallpaper patterns consist of "pie-cut" eyes. The elevators are cages made of "rib-bones" that groan as they ascend.
**Visual Design:** A towering, rickety Victorian structure that sags to the left as if it’s trying to listen to the ocean. Film grain is heavy here, creating a "snowy" static effect in dark corners. The windows look like mismatched, blinking eyes, and the front door is a gaping, toothless "mouth" with a welcome mat made of dried seaweed.
**Signature Gag Elements:** When a door closes, the doorknob turns into a literal "nose" that sneezes, blowing off hats and momentarily turning the room into a negative-exposure flash.

## Devil Reef Wharves
**The 1920s Archetype:** The Industrial Docklands.
**The Eldritch Twist:** The water isn't liquid; it’s a thick, churning black ink wash that occasionally "reaches" up with liquid hands to grab at the pier. The "fog" here has a physical weight, appearing as swirling, charcoal-smudged clouds that take the shape of screaming faces.
**Visual Design:** Rotting wooden piers that wiggle like spaghetti. The 16:9 frame sprocket holes appear "water-damaged" and "moldy." Devil Reef rises from the ink like a jagged, black tooth, with giant, rubbery shapes pulsating just beneath the surface.
**Signature Gag Elements:** A 2D "Ghost Ship" frequently sails past, but it’s just a "cardboard cutout" that flips over every few seconds, revealing it has no "back side"—just more black ink.
