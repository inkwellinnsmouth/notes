# The Path Into Innsmouth

## Archetype
The Rundown Roadside Tourist Trap & Bait Shop.

## The Eldritch Twist
The "tourist" attractions and fishing gear are thinly veiled cult paraphernalia. The "lobsters" in the traps have far too many joints and occasionally hum. The massive wooden "Welcome to Innsmouth" sign is carved to look like a friendly fish, but as you get closer, the wood grain swirls into terrified, screaming faces. 

## Visual Design
A winding, sagging dirt path lined with leaning, dilapidated wooden shacks selling "Fresh Bait" and "Souvenirs." The structures are rotting, warped, and top-heavy. The oppressive, heavy coastal fog rolls over the path, making it impossible to see further than the next shack. A single, crooked telephone pole sags under the weight of thick, dripping black wires that look suspiciously like tentacles.

## Signature Gag Elements
A rusty "turnstile" at the entrance to town that occasionally spins backward on its own, forcefully ejecting anyone who doesn't smell like fish. A souvenir snow globe where the tiny snow falls *up*, and a tiny eye blinks back at the viewer.
