**Name:** Barker Blot
**The 1920s Archetype:** The well-meaning, slightly clumsy Human "Everyman" (The Reluctant Ink-Demon)
**The Eldritch Twist:** He is literally made of "Abyssal Ink." When he gets scared, angry, or sneezes, his form loses its bouncy cohesion and briefly melts down into a horrifying, shifting puddle of eyes and jagged maws before he frantically scoops himself back together.
**Visual Design:** A classic 1920s cartoon HUMAN boy (think early Fleischer Studios human designs or a young Buster Keaton). He has a perfectly round, simple face with pie-cut eyes and a small button nose. No animal ears, no snout. He wears a slightly oversized, rumpled flat cap, suspenders over a collarless shirt, and oversized worn work boots. His body looks soft, slightly drooping, and heavily textured with charcoal/ink wash.
**Signature Animation Gag:** When terrified, his flat cap stays in mid-air while his body completely melts into a black puddle on the floor, leaving only his eyes darting back and forth before his body zips back up into place.
**Role in Innsmouth:** A plucky human everyman just trying to deliver goods, work part-time jobs, and survive a hostile universe he is vastly ill-equipped to understand. He can sometimes outsmart a deep one or a cultist through sheer cartoon elasticity, but is fundamentally powerless against the cosmic horrors of the setting.
