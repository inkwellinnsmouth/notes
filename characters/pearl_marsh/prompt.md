**Name:** Pearl Marsh
**The 1920s Archetype:** The Classic Beauty — a doe-eyed, glamorous woman in the tradition of 1920s Fleischer "Betty Boop" femininity. Graceful, charming, and disarmingly warm.
**The Eldritch Twist:** She is a **Deep One hybrid** from the Innsmouth line. In her human form she is considered classically beautiful by 1920s cartoon standards — large expressive eyes, elegant curves, stylish period dress. But under certain conditions (moonlight, saltwater, proximity to the sea, strong emotion) her Deep One nature surfaces: her skin takes on a faint iridescent sheen, her eyes widen into reflective fish-like orbs, gill slits appear along her neck, and her fingers lengthen into webbed claws. In full Deep One form she becomes a striking, disturbing fish-woman — still recognisably her, but unmistakably inhuman.
**Visual Design (Human Form):** A classically beautiful 1920s cartoon woman. Large, heavy-lashed pie-cut eyes, a small mouth, and a graceful neck. She wears period-appropriate dress — cloche hat, drop-waist dress or tailored coat. Rendered in the project's monotone charcoal/ink wash aesthetic with soft, rounded rubber-hose linework.
**Visual Design (Deep One Form):** The same silhouette but transformed — iridescent scales suggested by cross-hatching, bulging reflective eyes, visible gill slits, webbed hands. Her clothing remains but looks incongruous against the fish-woman body. She retains her grace and composure even in this form, which makes it more unsettling.
**Signature Animation Gag:** She sneezes — and for one frame her face is fully Deep One before snapping back to normal. She dabs her nose delicately with a handkerchief as if nothing happened.
**Role in Innsmouth:** A long-standing resident of Innsmouth with deep ties to its old families. She navigates both the human and Deep One worlds with practised ease, and may serve as an ambiguous ally — helpful and warm, but her ultimate loyalties are unclear. She is fully aware of what she is and has made peace with it.
