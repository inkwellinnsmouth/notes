# Barnaby Audio Notes

## Vocal Tone
- High-pitched, slightly raspy, cheerful "sailor boy" voice (think early Mickey Mouse meets Popeye, but without the mumbling).
- Very enthusiastic, even when talking about objectively terrifying things.
- Heavy use of 1920s slang ("Oh boy!", "Gee whiz!", "Well I'll be a salted herring!").

## Signature Sound Effects
- **Walking:** Squishy, rubbery "squeak-plop" sounds, like walking in wet boots that are slightly alive.
- **Startle Reaction:** A literal loud steamboat whistle (TOOT-TOOT!) overlapping with his scream.
- **Tentacle Arm Transformation:** Deep, wet, sloshing tearing sounds combined with a low-frequency, otherworldly bass hum.
- **Blinking:** Classic wooden block "clack-clack" for his normal eyes. A wet, squelchy "schlorp" for the eye on his anchor tattoo.
